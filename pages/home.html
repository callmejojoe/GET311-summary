What Is This Course?

Engineering economics is the analytical framework engineers use to make rational economic decisions. It combines financial mathematics with technical judgment to answer questions like: Which machine is cheaper to operate over its life? Should we buy or lease? Is this project financially viable?

At FUTM, GET 311 is a 3-unit compulsory course covering interest formulae, cash flow analysis, depreciation, break-even analysis, and investment comparison methods.

Why It Matters
"The engineer who understands economics makes decisions that last."
— Engineering Economics Principle

Every engineering decision — from selecting a pump to designing a factory layout — has economic consequences. The ability to quantify, compare, and justify these consequences is what separates a good engineer from a great one.
