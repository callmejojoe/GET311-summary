What Is an Annuity?

A uniform series of equal payments occurring at equal time intervals is called an annuity. The amounts must be identical (A each period), the intervals must be equal, and interest compounds at rate i. This simple structure appears in virtually every loan, savings plan, and lease encountered in engineering practice.

Example — Capital Recovery (Q12/Q50)

Generator costs ₦10,000. Repaid in 5 equal annual instalments at 8%. Find annual payment A.

A = P(A/P, 8%, 5) = 10,000 × 0.2505 = ₦2,504.57 ← Answer D // (A/P, 8%, 5) = 0.2505 from tables
Example — Series Compound Amount

Usman invests ₦700/month at 12% interest/month for 1 year. Total at end?

F = 700(F/A, 12%, 12) = 700×[(1.12)¹²−1]/0.12 = 700×[(3.896−1)/0.12] = 700 × 24.133 = ₦16,893.10
Example — Sinking Fund (Q13)

What annuity over 12 years accumulates ₦20,000 at 6% annually?

A = F(A/F, 6%, 12) = 20,000 × 0.05928 = ₦1,185.54 ← Answer B
⚑ Common Pitfalls — Annuities
  • Ordinary vs Annuity Due: Default is ordinary (end-of-period). If payments are at the beginning, multiply the ordinary annuity result by (1+i).
  • Deferred annuity n confusion: The n in (P/A) = number of payments, not total time span. If 8 payments start at year 10, n=8, D=10.
  • Sinking fund vs Capital Recovery: Both find A, but (A/F) is given F; (A/P) is given P. They are NOT interchangeable — always check what you're given.
Self-Assessment · Annuities
Q1. An annuity where payments are made at the BEGINNING of each period is called:
A. Ordinary annuity
B. Deferred annuity
C. Annuity due
D. Perpetuity
✓ Answer: C — Annuity due. Beginning-of-period payments = annuity due. End-of-period = ordinary annuity. Q80 in the past paper.
Q2. Present worth of a ₦3,000 annuity at 8% compounded annually, paid for 3 years?
A. ₦7,654.04
B. ₦7,731.29
C. ₦7,420.89
D. ₦7,590.12
✓ Answer: B — ₦7,731.29. P = 3000(P/A, 8%, 3) = 3000 × 2.5771 = ₦7,731.30. This is Q11 and Q51 from the past paper.
Q3. An annuity that continues forever with no fixed end date is called:
A. Annuity certain
B. Perpetuity
C. Deferred annuity
D. Ordinary annuity
✓ Answer: B — Perpetuity. P = A/i for a perpetuity. Q81 in the past paper.
